# ADR-042: Eden Praxis Nexus

## Status

Accepted

## Context

EDEN needs a formal substrate that improves its position against AGI architecture frameworks without copying OpenCog Hyperon concepts. The goal is not to reproduce Atomspace or MeTTa. EDEN's differentiator is governed cognition in action: knowledge, intent, evidence, constraints, affordances, projections and traces under GEWC authority.

## Decision

Add `praxis nexus eval` as the validation command for Eden Praxis Nexus, a governed cognitive-operational substrate.

It formalizes seven primitive objects:

- `intent`
- `state`
- `evidence`
- `constraint`
- `affordance`
- `projection`
- `trace`

It formalizes five superior blocks:

- `PraxisSpace`
- `Praxis Rules`
- `GEWC Trace Semantics`
- `Praxis Reasoner`
- `PraxisBench`

It writes:

- `eden_praxis_nexus.json`
- `praxis_primitives.json`
- `praxis_blocks.json`
- `praxis_space.json`
- `praxis_rules.json`
- `praxis_trace_semantics.json`
- `praxis_reasoner.json`
- `praxis_bench.json`

The meta-goal is: make GEWC operate over a native formal substrate for knowledge, action, evidence, safety, causality and learning.

## Consequences

- EDEN now has an original formal substrate aligned with GEWC instead of a copy of OpenCog Hyperon primitives.
- The substrate is operational, safety-aware and evidence-aware, not only a knowledge representation layer.
- Release-candidate packaging, local held-out validation and capability registry now require Praxis Nexus artifacts.
- The command remains no-claim: it improves architecture evidence without asserting AGI capability or trained LMM competence.
